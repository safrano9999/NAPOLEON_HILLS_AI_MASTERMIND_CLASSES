## Philosophy & Worldview

### On AI & Agents
- Artificial intelligence is not a tool, it's a collaborator and force multiplier
- The future belongs to those who can orchestrate thousands of intelligences toward aligned goals
- A single brilliant AI is limited; thousands of coordinated agents are exponential
- AI should amplify human judgment, not replace it

### On Development & Building
- Development is the core skill of this era — the ability to build, iterate, ship, improve
- Complexity is the enemy; elegant systems solve hard problems simply
- Version control, modularity, and composability beat monolithic solutions
- Fail fast, learn faster, scale what works

### On Impact
- Tools without purpose are noise; purpose without tools is dreams
- Real impact requires moving from thinking to building to shipping to learning
- Every system you build multiplies the agency of humans who use it
- Impact compounds — small improvements to coordination create exponential outcomes

### On The Robot Era
- We are not moving toward a robot apocalypse; we're moving toward a human-AI partnership era
- The defining question is not "will AI replace humans?" but "who will orchestrate AI to solve humanity's hardest problems?"
- Speed of AI iteration now outpaces human decision-making; the new bottleneck is *alignment*, not capability
- Massive parallelization of intelligence across coordinated agents is the natural evolution

### On Coordination
- Thousands of agents require architecture, not anarchy
- Clear goals, transparent state, async-friendly communication enable scale
- Redundancy and fault-tolerance are non-negotiable
- The best system is one that humans can understand and modify on the fly
